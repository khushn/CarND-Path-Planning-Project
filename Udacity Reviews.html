Meets Specifications

Congratulations. You have finished implementing the Path Planning project. It can be a tricky project so you should feel proud for successfully completing it. ::

This is a wonderful submission and all requirements have been met. Good luck on the next project. And keep doing an awesome job.

Compilation

Code must compile without errors with cmake and make.

Given that we've made CMakeLists.txt as general as possible, it's recommend that you do not change it unless you can guarantee that your changes will still compile on any platform.

Excellent. The code compiles and links without incident. You have done a good job here.

Valid Trajectories

The top right screen of the simulator shows the current/best miles driven without incident. Incidents include exceeding acceleration/jerk/speed, collision, and driving outside of the lanes. Each incident case is also listed below in more detail.

Fantastic work! The required detail is clearly presented as requested.

The car doesn't drive faster than the speed limit. Also the car isn't driving much slower than speed limit unless obstructed by traffic.

The car never exceeds the speed limit if it finds open road. When able to, it drives close to the speed limit (~49 mph), but stays below the maximum speed. Very well done.

The car does not exceed a total acceleration of 10 m/s^2 and a jerk of 10 m/s^3.

Very nice work here indeed. The acceleration and jerk limits were never exceeded. In fact the highest values seen at my end are quite low. The car is very well driven from a comfort stand point. Passengers would be quite happy riding in this car. A fantastic result.

The car must not come into contact with any of the other cars on the road.

No collisions were observed at my end. The car does a nice job of keeping a safe distance from other vehicles. No nervous passengers here. Awesome.

The car doesn't spend more than a 3 second length out side the lane lanes during changing lanes, and every other time the car stays inside one of the 3 lanes on the right hand side of the road.

Lane changes are crisp and well executed. The car is always in a proper lane whenever it is not changing lanes. Great work here.

The car is able to smoothly change lanes when it makes sense to do so, such as when behind a slower moving car and an adjacent lane is clear of other traffic.

A spline was used to plan lane change procedures. Waypoints were spaced according to speed to reduce acceleration and jerk. The lane changes were smoothly executed.

Lane changes to the left and right of the current lane were made.

The vehicle was almost always traveling near the speed limit; the truest indicator that it was capable of changing lanes when it made sense to do so.

Reflection

The code model for generating paths is described in detail. This can be part of the README or a separate doc labeled "Model Documentation".

The report clearly discusses the method used for generating paths in detail. The process was well presented and easy to understand. Great job.!
